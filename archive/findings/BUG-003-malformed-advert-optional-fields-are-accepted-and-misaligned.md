## TL;DR

OpenHop does not consistently prove that every flag-controlled advert field is fully present before consuming it. A truncated field can shift subsequent interpretation or be silently accepted. To fix it, make advert parsing transactional: validate the complete length for each enabled field, reject on the first short segment, and only publish a Contact after the entire payload is valid. Add truncation tests at every byte boundary.

**Current status: ✅ Fully fixed.** Advert parsing now computes and validates the complete flag-dependent minimum length before consuming optional fields, and a contact is published only after that parse succeeds. The transactional bounds check is implemented in [parse_advert_appdata](https://github.com/openhop-dev/openhop_core/blob/fix/all-the-things-core/src/openhop_core/protocol/utils.py#L115-L148). ## What happens

OpenHop does not consistently prove that every flag-controlled advert field is fully present before consuming it. A truncated field can shift subsequent interpretation or be silently accepted.

## How official MeshCore handles it

AdvertDataParser advances over every flag-required fixed field and marks the result invalid when app_data_len is shorter than the resulting offset. It does not reinterpret bytes from a truncated earlier field as a later name.

## How the OpenHop stack handles it

**OpenHop Core:** The Python parser conditionally slices fields and can continue with short slices or an altered offset rather than rejecting the entire advert.

## What needs to change

Make advert parsing transactional: validate the complete length for each enabled field, reject on the first short segment, and only publish a Contact after the entire payload is valid. Add truncation tests at every byte boundary.
