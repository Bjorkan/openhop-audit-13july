# BUG-122 — Flood forwarding retransmits RAW_CUSTOM and unknown payload types

## TL;DR

The Repeater sends every valid non-multipart flood packet through its generic forwarding path, including RAW_CUSTOM and unknown payload-type values. Official MeshCore explicitly keeps RAW_CUSTOM local/direct and does not flood-route unknown types. OpenHop can therefore amplify experimental, malformed or future payloads across a mesh when firmware nodes would stop them.

## What happens

The Repeater sends every valid non-multipart flood packet through its generic forwarding path, including RAW_CUSTOM and unknown payload-type values. Official MeshCore explicitly keeps RAW_CUSTOM local/direct and does not flood-route unknown types. OpenHop can therefore amplify experimental, malformed or future payloads across a mesh when firmware nodes would stop them.

## How official MeshCore handles it

Mesh.cpp handles RAW_CUSTOM only for direct delivery and comments out flood routing. Its default payload branch logs unknown types and deliberately does not call `routeRecvPacket()`.

## How the OpenHop stack handles it

`process_packet()` special-cases only MULTIPART, then sends all flood routes to `flood_forward()`. That function validates route/path policy but contains no payload allowlist or RAW_CUSTOM/unknown suppression.

## What needs to change

Mirror the firmware payload dispatch boundary before generic flood forwarding. Suppress RAW_CUSTOM and unknown/reserved payload types while retaining firmware-routed types. Keep direct intermediate forwarding semantics separate, and add exhaustive tests over all 16 payload type nibbles and all route variants.
