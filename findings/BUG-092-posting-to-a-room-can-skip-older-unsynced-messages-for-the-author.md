# BUG-092 — Posting to a room can skip older unsynced messages for the author

## TL;DR

After accepting a post, OpenHop advances the author's global `sync_since` watermark to the new post's timestamp to avoid echoing it. If another author's older post is still unsynced, that older post is now below the watermark and is permanently skipped. MeshCore avoids self-echo per post by comparing authors and does not advance the watermark merely because the client posted.

## What happens

After accepting a post, OpenHop advances the author's global `sync_since` watermark to the new post's timestamp to avoid echoing it. If another author's older post is still unsynced, that older post is now below the watermark and is permanently skipped. MeshCore avoids self-echo per post by comparing authors and does not advance the watermark merely because the client posted.

## How official MeshCore handles it

The room loop selects posts newer than the client's acknowledged watermark and separately excludes posts whose author matches the client. The watermark advances only after a delivery ACK.

## How the OpenHop stack handles it

`add_post()` writes the new timestamp into the author's sync row. SQLite later queries only other-author posts with `post_timestamp > sync_since`, so unrelated older posts disappear from that client's future result set.

## What needs to change

Do not advance `sync_since` on author insertion. Keep self-exclusion in the unsynced query and advance the watermark only after acknowledged delivery. Add a regression test where a client has an older pending post, creates a newer post, and must still receive the older one.
