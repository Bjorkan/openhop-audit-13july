# BUG-106 — Incoming plain and signed messages do not refresh contact lastmod

## TL;DR

After authenticating an incoming plain or signed direct message, OpenHop never refreshes the sender contact’s `lastmod`. Official MeshCore updates `lastmod` to the local receive time for both message classes. Active peers can therefore continue to look stale in contact synchronization, eviction and UI logic even while they are sending messages.

## What happens

After authenticating an incoming plain or signed direct message, OpenHop never refreshes the sender contact’s `lastmod`. Official MeshCore updates `lastmod` to the local receive time for both message classes. Active peers can therefore continue to look stale in contact synchronization, eviction and UI logic even while they are sending messages.

## How official MeshCore handles it

For `TXT_TYPE_PLAIN` and `TXT_TYPE_SIGNED_PLAIN`, `BaseChatMesh` sets `from.lastmod` from the local RTC before invoking the application callback. Signed messages separately advance `sync_since` from the sender timestamp.

## How the OpenHop stack handles it

The text handler advances `sync_since` for signed traffic but has no `lastmod` assignment after successful authentication. Its event and `packet.decrypted` output do not update or persist the contact’s local activity timestamp.

## What needs to change

Refresh `lastmod` only after successful authentication and supported-type validation for plain and signed messages, using the local unique/current clock rather than the sender timestamp. Update the backing contact store and persistence hook, and test that CLI/unsupported text does not incorrectly change it.
