# BUG-039 — SEND_CONFIRMED always reports zero trip time

## TL;DR

The final four bytes of PUSH_CODE_SEND_CONFIRMED are the elapsed milliseconds from send to ACK. OpenHop hardcodes them to zero, removing information expected by companion clients. To fix it, record a monotonic send timestamp with each expected ACK and calculate the unsigned elapsed milliseconds on confirmation. Clear the same entry after emitting the push.

## What happens

The final four bytes of PUSH_CODE_SEND_CONFIRMED are the elapsed milliseconds from send to ACK. OpenHop hardcodes them to zero, removing information expected by companion clients.

## How official MeshCore handles it

processAck subtracts expected_ack_table[i].msg_sent from the current monotonic millisecond clock and writes the resulting trip_time.

## How the OpenHop stack handles it

**OpenHop Core:** _push._on_send_confirmed packs the ACK followed by integer zero, and pending ACK state stores no send time.

## What needs to change

Record a monotonic send timestamp with each expected ACK and calculate the unsigned elapsed milliseconds on confirmation. Clear the same entry after emitting the push.
